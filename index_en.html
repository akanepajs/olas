Cage-free share of egg listings at Latvian retailers

Online catalogue snapshot by SKU. Latest data: 2026-06-26 (updated daily).
Latvia's large online grocers list almost only cage-free eggs by stock-keeping unit (SKU) count: Rimi 93%, Barbora (Maxima) 100%, Lidl 100%. That is well above the national cage-free production share (about 47% of laying-hen places; Eglitis and Kanepajs 2026). This is a shelf-presence indicator, not a sales-volume share.

Price per egg

Price per egg (pack price divided by the number of eggs per pack, so 6- and 10-egg packs compare fairly). Organic eggs are the dearest; Lidl's barn eggs are the cheapest overall. Medians: Rimi 0.33 EUR/egg, Barbora 0.30 EUR/egg, Lidl 0.18 EUR/egg.

Method

Every chicken shell-egg listing in each retailer's online catalogue was read and classified by EU production code (0 organic, 1 free-range, 2 barn, 3 caged), using the egg-marking code in the name (Nr.0/1/2/3) and/or production keywords (kuti detas = barn, sprostos = caged, brivas turesanas = free-range, eko/bio = organic). Quail eggs and egg products (egg white, etc.) were excluded. Cage-free % = codes 0/1/2 over all chicken shell-egg listings. Every listing carried a production label (0 unknowns), so the national-anchor adjustment leaves the figure unchanged.

Coverage

Of six large chains, three have a machine-readable online egg catalogue (Rimi, Barbora/Maxima, and Lidl; Lidl carries a small egg range). The others:

  • top!: top! (etop.lv) is TOP's own-brand (TIP TOP) showcase, not a full online grocery: the eggs section has only one own-brand product (TIP TOP barn eggs, EU code 2, about 0.30 EUR/egg), so it is not comparable to a full assortment and is excluded.
  • Mego: Mego (mego.lv) has no online store (informational site only); no egg catalogue.
  • Elvi: Elvi (elvi.lv) eggs page lists no shoppable products (WordPress promo/catalogue site); no listing-level data.

Limitations

  • Shelf presence by SKU count, not sales volume. A retailer with 90% cage-free SKUs could still sell many caged eggs if the cheap, high-volume lines are caged.
  • The 47% reference is production capacity (laying-hen places), not retail volume.
  • Price is the listed price (may include promotions) divided by egg count; not weighted by sales volume.

Sources

  • Scraped online catalogues: Rimi, Barbora, Lidl.
  • Eglitis and Kanepajs (2026), Economic analysis of a laying-hen cage ban in Latvia (national cage-free share).
  • European Commission, egg marketing standards (codes 0/1/2/3).
